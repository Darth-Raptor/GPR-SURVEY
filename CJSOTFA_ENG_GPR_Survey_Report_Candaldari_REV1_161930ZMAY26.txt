1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Helicopter-borne ground-penetrating radar collection over the designated Candaldari District scan area produced a semi-successful subsurface survey. The collection identified one high-confidence primary subsurface corridor, two moderate-confidence associated branch features, and several low-confidence anomalies consistent with either tunnel extensions, ventilation/access infrastructure, disturbed overburden, drainage voids, or natural subsurface discontinuities.

The strongest return is spatially consistent with a probable man-made subsurface corridor extending from the E1 vicinity through the E2 vicinity toward the E3 vicinity. E1, E2, and E3 are the only confirmed player-facing access references carried in this report. Additional detected points are not treated as confirmed entrances. They are reported as suspected subsurface nodes, thermal anomalies, ventilation candidates, or unresolved features pending ground exploitation.

Satellite thermal imaging collected at 170400ZMAY26 identified localized thermal anomalies along the GPR-derived corridor. The strongest thermal correlation was observed at E1 and E3, with weaker but relevant indicators along the assessed corridor and near possible lateral features. Thermal results support the presence of air exchange or recent surface activity at selected locations but do not independently confirm the full tunnel configuration.

The survey does not resolve the entire network. The product should be treated as an engineer cueing product and exploitation aid, not as a complete underground route map.

4. GPR FINDINGS

A. A-01 PRIMARY CORRIDOR

The strongest GPR return is a continuous, irregular, northeast-trending subsurface anomaly beginning near E1, passing through the E2 vicinity, and extending toward E3. The return displays characteristics consistent with a linear void, disturbed-overburden zone, or engineered subsurface passage.

Assessed corridor:
- Start: E1 vicinity / grid 2455-1012
- Intermediate known reference: E2 vicinity / grid 2537-0993
- Northeast endpoint: E3 vicinity / grid 2513-1094
- Observed trace length: approximately 1.8-2.0 km
- General orientation: southwest to northeast with irregular curvature
- Confidence: HIGH for subsurface corridor presence; MODERATE for exact continuity and internal configuration

Assessment:
A-01 is assessed as the principal detected tunnel feature. The return is strong enough to support a probable man-made subsurface route connecting the E1/E2 southern access area with the E3 northeastern access area. Exact width, chambers, internal junctions, serviceability, and trap status remain unconfirmed.

B. A-02 SOUTHERN NODE CLUSTER

A compact cluster of secondary returns was detected near and east of the E1/E2 portion of the primary corridor. These returns are not assessed as confirmed entrances. They may represent short lateral cuts, covered shafts, ventilation infrastructure, shallow utility-style cuts, disturbed backfill, or local widening of the primary tunnel.

Assessed feature:
- General area: between grids 2486-0994 and 2529-1026
- Relationship: adjacent to or overlapping the southern half of A-01
- Observed trace character: discontinuous short returns with mixed thermal support
- Confidence: MODERATE for subsurface disturbance; LOW TO MODERATE for function

Assessment:
A-02 should be treated as a suspected node cluster associated with the main corridor. It is not sufficient to brief as a confirmed entrance cluster. Ground forces should prioritize the area only after E1, E2, and E3 are secured or observed.

C. A-03 EASTERN LATERAL FEATURE

A moderate-strength GPR return extends east from the E1/A-02 area toward a suspected lateral endpoint east of the known access corridor. The return is shorter and less continuous than A-01, but is consistent with a lateral branch, service cut, ventilation run, or older tunnel section.

Assessed corridor:

- Observed trace length: approximately 700-800 m

- Confidence: MODERATE

Assessment:
A-03 is assessed as a probable lateral subsurface feature linked to the main access area. Thermal support in the endpoint area suggests possible ventilation or disturbed ground, but the location should be reported as a suspected node, not a confirmed entrance.

D. A-04 NORTHERN BRANCH / E3 APPROACH

7. ENGINEER LIMITATIONS

A. GPR collection identified subsurface anomalies, not a complete tunnel map.

B. GPR cannot determine personnel presence, booby traps, internal chamber layout, door status, or exact tunnel serviceability.

C. Thermal collection does not see through soil or rock. It only indicates surface heat-transfer, airflow, disturbed ground, or recent-use signatures.

D. Low-confidence anomalies may represent drainage, natural voids, buried debris, backfill, rock fractures, or non-tunnel excavation.

E. E1, E2, and E3 are the only confirmed access references in this product. Other locations require physical confirmation before being briefed as entrances or exits.

8. FOLLOW-ON ENGINEER RECOMMENDATIONS

A. Prioritize ground confirmation at E1, E2, and E3.

B. Establish observation or cordon coverage over A-02 and A-04 before entering any known access point.

C. Treat all suspected access or ventilation nodes as potentially booby-trapped.

D. Use EOD, confined-space procedures, and remote inspection where available.

E. Conduct follow-on thermal or small-UAS observation of A-02/A-03 endpoint areas during pre-dawn or night periods.

F. Do not publish A-05 or A-06 as confirmed tunnel branches without physical confirmation or corroborating reporting.

9. OVERALL ENGINEER ASSESSMENT

The GPR and thermal survey was semi-successful. It did not fully resolve the underground system, but it produced a credible cueing picture. The A-01 E1-E2-E3 corridor is assessed as a probable man-made subsurface route. A-02, A-03, and A-04 represent plausible associated branches or support nodes. A-05 and A-06 remain unresolved.

The recommended exploitation posture is to treat E1, E2, and E3 as priority access/control points, secure or observe the suspected node areas, and avoid assuming that the absence of strong GPR/thermal return equals absence of tunnel infrastructure.
